Paul Brown

The man who re-invented football
Paul Brown was a constant innovator and teacher. He is one of the legendary coaches of the NFL.

    Timeline of Paul's NFL accomplishments:

  • 1946-1949 - Paul coaches the Cleveland Browns in the AAFC
    • The Cleveland Browns were created and named after Paul even though he protested the name.
    • Won the AAFC all four years, effectively killing the AAFC due to lack of competitive games. The Browns then merged into the NFL.
  • 1950-1963 - Paul coaches the Cleveland Browns in the NFL
    • Won the NFL Championship in the 1950, 1954, and 1955 seasons.
    • Paul left the Browns after a nasty power struggle with Art Modell.
  • 1968-1975 - Paul coaches the Cincinnati Bengals
    • The Bengals went from starting a franchise to making the postseason three times in Paul's time as head coach, never winning a playoff game.

"I enjoy winning and very much dislike losing- but I did not allow either of them to obsess me. I was a silent loser, believing that if you won you said little, and if you lost you said even less."

-- Paul Brown
